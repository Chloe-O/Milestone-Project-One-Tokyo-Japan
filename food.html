Cheap but authentic Tokyo eats

3 of the best things to eat in Tokyo, jump to:
Sushi | Ramen | Curry

Sushi

Sushi is undeniably the food most associated with Japan, the seafood in Japan is some of the freshest and most high quality whilst being reasonably priced.

Our recommendation for sushi is the very family friendly chain Kura Sushi. Cheap, suitable for those who enjoy seafood and with plenty of options for those who don't. On top of having conveyor belt service, there is also a chance to win a free gift with each five plates finished via a capsule machine

Kura Sushi has chains all over Japan with 36 restaurants in Tokyo alone. Kura Sushi's website contains lots of information about their menu and locations

Ramen

Another must-try food whilst in Japan, Ramen. A hearty noodle soup comprised of wheat noodles, a flavourful soup broth and meat, seafood or vegetables. Not to be confused with the instant noodles you might be used to seeing in the supermarket.

There are ramen restaurants at almost every turn in Tokyo, each serving different but traditional takes on the dish.

Ichiran Ramen is a popular chain, with restaurants all over Japan, which serves a small selection of ramen including their speciality Tonkotsu Ramen which is made up of a rich pork bone soup.

Curry

Not the first food you might think of when thinking about Japanese food but definitely worth a try. Japanese curry is much more mild than it's Indian counterpart but still just as delicious. The dish is a common home cooked meal usually made with chicken, beef or pork and served with rice.

Our favourite curry was in the popular chain CoCo Curry in Shinjuku, popular all over Japan and has recently opened its first store in London.

This chain is so popular as it offers an almost infinite combination of curry toppings such as deep fried meats, seafood, vegetable and even sausage. CoCo Curry's website explains how to order - First choose how much rice you'd like, the spice-level of the curry sauce then finally your toppings. Our favourite was spicy curry with fried chicken, topped with cheese. Don't knock it until you've tried it!
