Sushi

Sushi is undeniably the food most associated with Japan, the seafood in Japan is some of the freshest and most high quality whilst being reasonably priced.

Our recommendation for sushi is the very family friendly chain Kura Sushi. Cheap, suitable for those who enjoy seafood and with plenty of options for those who don't. On top of having conveyor belt service, there is also a chance to win a free gift with each five plates finished via a capsule machine

Kura Sushi has chains all over Japan with 36 restaurants in Tokyo alone. Kura Sushi's website contains lots of information about their menu and locations

Sensoji is the oldest Buddist temple in Asakusa, Tokyo believed to have been built in the year 645. It receives around 30 million visits each year. making it one of the most popular temples for tourists to visit.

The Temple is made up of the Main Hall, Kaminari-mon Gate, Nakamise Shopping Street among other gates and halls.

Sensoji is free to visit and contains many small shops selling traditional Japanese street food, clothing and souvenirs, many fesitivals and events take place at the site each year. The official Sensoji website contains an abundance of information about the site.

Whilst not strictly Tokyo, the sacred Mt. Fuji can easily be reached in less than 2 hours from the centre of Tokyo using the Shinkansen or 'bullet train'. Mt. Fuji is synonymous with Japan and a must see when visiting.

Mt. Fuji is Japan's highest mountain at 3776 metres and can be seen from Tokyo on clear days.There are many small towns surrounding Mt. Fuji such as Hakone and Fujigoko which offer great views and lake cruises
